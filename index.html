The Data Behind the Shift

The Core Insight The story of San Francisco over the last twenty years is often told through the lens of a "Tech Boom," but the police data tells a more complicated story of displacement. As once-industrial districts like SoMa and working-class hubs like The Mission transformed into high-rent corridors, the "footprint" of criminal incidents didn't simply shrink—it migrated. Our analysis reveals that while "street-level" crime appears to vanish from newly gentrified blocks, it often reappears with higher density in the city's remaining low-income pockets and outer-edge neighborhoods. We aren't necessarily seeing a "safer" city; we are seeing a city where the "border" of high-incident zones has been squeezed and pushed along economic fault lines.
